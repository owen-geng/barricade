List of optimisations made to improve performance:

Environment:
Algorithmic (maxflow) optimisations to improve valid move find times. Cuts
required calculations down dramatically as we are no longer (always) calling
a BFS on every possible barricade.

JIT compiled many heavy functions to improve runtime - cost of compilation is
high, but it gets cached afterwards and runs at near C speed.


DQN Agent:
torch.compile(). small network though, so not much performance change.

Train every 4 steps instead of every step, reduces number of backward passes.

N-step returns. Speeds up training in early steps as Q-values are very noisy,
so it increases the ratio between reward signal and noise.

Instead of training 2 agents simultaneously, which lead to strategies converging
to ones that simply beat each other (overfitting to two non-optimal strategies),
changed to train against a fully random opponent for initial boostrapping, before
switching to more complex self-play. Pooling to play against random past iterations
of itself to prevent overfitting to a singular strategy.

Cycle prevention. Early on in training, since initialised Q networks are highly
random, it often gets trapped in infinite cycles. Later on, after the agents have
learned some strategy, then backtracking may be viable, so the penalty is weaned off.

Need to flip the board depending on who's turn it is. The agent loses track of the
general direction it wants to travel in otherwise.
